General model for hypotheses on autism (inspired from Rubenstein2003)

    1. Precoce genetic or environmental alteration (gene mutation or atypical environment)
    1. Less GABAergic neurons in cortex/amygdale (less produced or migrated)
    1. Less inhibition (GABA) compared to excitation (glutamate) = déséquilibre excitation/inhibition
    1. Excessive Plasticity (too much LTP, freezed circuits) -> learning too fast and less flexible, overreactivity to stimuli
    1. underdifferentiated representations, simple stimuli activate large populations of neurons
    1. Local excess excitability -> less differentiation -> high distance lack of excitability -> lower connectivity
    1. Atypical behavior like in autism (hypersensitivity, rigid memory, less flexibility, increased risk of epilepsy)

    1. Less GABAergic neurons in cortex/amygdale (less produced or migrated)
    1. Less inhibition (GABA) compared to excitation (glutamate) = déséquilibre excitation/inhibition
    1. Excessive Plasticity (too much LTP, freezed circuits) -> learning too fast and less flexible, overreactivity to stimuli
    1. underdifferentiated representations, simple stimuli activate large populations of neurons
