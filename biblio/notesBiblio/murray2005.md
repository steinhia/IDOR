## Introduction 
* monotropism : low range of interests -> bassins of attraction
* competition for attention shapes cognitive processes
    * social interactions, language etc all tasks that require broader use of attention
    * activities inhibites by the canalization of interests into few highly aroused interests (opposed to polytropism)
* To perform a task, must :
  * understand the goal
  * have interest
  * know how to perform the task
  * be able to perform the tas
  * 4 steps maybe impaired in ASD
* experiments distress in failure / hates to be corrected
* lack of generalization
  * hyperawareness within the attentinal tunnel, hypoawareness outside it
* top-down processing strict/limited/resistent to correction
* lack of preparedness -> hypersensibility
* greater variability among ASD than controls -> diff in research
* deficit in theory of mind -> more diff in real-life, because cues occur rapidly
