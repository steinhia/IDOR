## Attention from a resource perspective
* it has been proposed that each cerebral hemisphere has a functionally distinct set of resources (herdman, 1985)
* almost all non-language tasks can be performed by either hemisphere
* the performance of more computationally complex tasks is aided by interhemispheric interaction (across different modalities)
    * parallel processing
* split-brain patients cannot compare letters in 2 different hemispheres -> assume it goes through the CC
* So IHI measured through difference unilateral/bilateral conditions
* depending on computational complexity (complex perceptual processing or decision)
* always interaction complexity/laterality in a huge variety of visual/auditory/tactile tasks
    * Thus, we believe that interhemispheric interaction is a means for modulating the attentional capacity of the brain
    * but non for language tasks
    * different parts of the CC -> general property of the CC
* when detection or OR or AND (color and shape), AND can do work in //, OR does not need both processing in // 
    * -> hyp : divided trials (color in one field, shape in other) greater advantage for AND
    * studies show advantage thanks to division of processing rather than division of input (conditions for divisions of input and processing tasks)

## A ROLE FOR INTERHEMISPHERIC INTERACTION IN ATTENTIONAL FUNCTIONING: CONVERGING EVIDENCE
* split-brain patients have attentional disorder / diff in dual tasks
    * suggest that the callosum normally acts as a mechanism for dispersing large processing loads across the hemispheres
* Stroop with name/color patch -> no stroop effect in bilateral conditions for split-brains
* various CC problems -> attention problems
* ADHD -> CC abnormlities
* --> support for the idea that interhemispheric processing via the callosum affects attentional functioning
