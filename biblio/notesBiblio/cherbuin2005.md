# Practice makes two hemispheres almost perfect
 
## Resume 
* inter-hemispheric interactions (IHI) can be measured by bilateral distribution advantage (BDA) 
* decrease in BDA with practice / improvement in unilateral but far less in bilateral condition
* seems linked to general practice effect (intra-hemispheric) rather than priming effect
 
## Introduction 
* inter-hemispheric interactions (IHI) important for the efficient recruitment of limited resources in heavy load situations
* also selective attention modulated by IHI
* old age : assist better use of decreasing cognitive resources
* inter-hemispheric transfer (IHT) takes time (from 2 to 45 ms according to complexity)
* performance can be better when forced to use 2 hemispheres, particularly when complex
    * a/a or A/a in same/different hemispheres A/a double better
    * bilateral distribution advantage (BDA) to measure IHI
* decrease in BDA with practice (Liedermann1985)
    * weissman2003 : unilateral improved more than bilateral trials
    * could be due to shift of strategy (algorithmic->memory), 
        * less complexity -> more single hemisphere
        * but would expect both unilateral and bilateral to improve
    * practice effects more at the intra-hemispheric level ? (bilateral slower to improve ?)
    * priming less efficient for bilateral trials ?
 
## Etude 
* hyp : study with more trials, would find a more general practice effect for first trial of some kind, no priming effect
* reduction BDA
* to compare general practice/priming effects : k-trial within each block
    * general practice, should already have improved for the last block
    * priming effect, effect of number of exposures only
* found effect of general practice, but no priming effect
    * unsurprising : letter identification task, many combinations, should not leave clear memory traces
* neurophysiological origins of the general-practice effect : 
  * plasticity ?
  * subprocesses bette synchronized ? (bottleneck disappears or just decreases in size)
 
## Discussion
