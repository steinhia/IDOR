# Decreased Interhemispheric Functional Connectivity in Autism
 
## Resume 
* The cortical underconnectivity theory asserts that reduced long-range functional connectivity might contribute to a neural mechanism for autism. 
* Observed interhemispheric connectivity differences were better explained by diagnosis of autism than by potentially confounding neuropsychological metrics of language, IQ, or handedness.
* Although both corpus callosal volume and gray matter interhemispheric connectivity were significantly reduced in autism, no direct relationship was observed between them, suggesting that structural and functional metrics measure different aspects of interhemispheric connectivity.
* Autism subjects continue to show developmental differences in interhemispheric connectivity into early adulthood.
