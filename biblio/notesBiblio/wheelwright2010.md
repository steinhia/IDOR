# Defining the broader, medium and narrow autism phenotype among parents using the Autism Spectrum Quotient (AQ)
 
## Resume 
* broader autism phenotype (BAP) BAP is milder but qualitatively similar
 
## Introduction 
* Proposition : Autism Spectrum Quotient (AQ)
* QTL : Quantitative traits loci
* Quantitative traits are characteristics that can be associated with a particular condition but are also continuously distributed in the non-affected population.
* 4 instruments available for the assessment of the BAP: 
    * the Family History Interview (FHI), developed by Rutter and Folstein; 
    * the Social Responsiveness Scale (SRS) [11]; (65 item questionnaire)
    * the Broader Phenotype Autism Symptom Scale (BPASS) [12]; (semi-structured interview)
    * the Broad Autism Phenotype Questionnaire (BAPQ) [13] (36 items within aloof personality, pragmatic language deficits, and rigid personality)
 
## Etude 
* desired properties for a tool : 
    * (1) : quantitative, avoiding ceiling and floor effects
    * (2) able to distinguish control/ASD
    * (3) same material for different ages
    * (4) should produce near-normal distribution
    * (5) good test-retest reliability
    * (6) good inter-cultural applicability
    * (7) should correlate with physiological measures/psychological measures (concurrent validity)
    * (8) quick and easy to use
* FHI and BPASS are not quantitative, and time-consuming
* SRS not normally distributed
* BAPQ limited to adults
* Proposition : Autism Spectrum Quotient (AQ)
* self-report, forced-choice questionnaire and is at the reading level of a typical 10-year-old
* 50 items, covering behaviours across five domains: communication, social skills, attention switching, imagination, and attention to detail.
* 4-point scale : strongly agrees or disagrees -> binary or four-point
* meets criterion
* tests
  * NAP : narrow autism phenotype (>3sd)
  * BAP : broad AP (>1sd)
  * MAP : medium AP (>2sd)
  * on patients and parents
  * 1582 families, 571 fathers and 1421 mothers, 418 couples
  * 727 were reported to have autism, 725 to have Asperger's syndrome (AS), 185 to have high functioning autism (HFA) and 115 to have pervasive development disorder
  * parents of children with ASD scored higher than parents of controls, more BAP, MAP and NAP
