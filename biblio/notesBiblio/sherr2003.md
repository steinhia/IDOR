# The ARX story (epilepsy, mental retardation, autism, and cerebral malformations): one gene leads to many phenotypes

## Resume 
* Infantile spasms, mental retardation, autism, and dystonia represent disabling diseases for which little etiologic information is available. Mutations in the Aristaless related homeobox gene (ARX) have been found in patients with these conditions.
* Mutations in the homeobox gene, ARX, cause a diverse spectrum of disease that includes cognitive impairment, epilepsy, and in another group of patients severe cortical malformations. Although the precise prevalence of ARX mutations is unclear, ARX may rival Fragile X as a cause of mental retardation and epilepsy in males
 
## Introduction 
* homeobox genes : control the expression of other genes, organize the development of embryon
    * mutations associated with several cerebral malformations
* ARX (Aristaless Related Homeobox) : critical for brain and neuron development
    * have been shown to be responsible for diffuse central nervous system malformations
        * lissencephaly (no circonvolution)
        * AgCC
        * thalamic/midbrain dysplasia
        * also found in patients with West syndrom (infantile spasms with hypsarrhythmia and mental retardation), epilepsy, autism...
        * In autism, no cortical malformation -> more subtle role in neurone differentiation

* Lissencephaly
    * co-occurrence of X-linked lissencephaly, agenesis of the corpus callosum, and microphallus, in several families
    * new borns had onset of intractable epilepsy, hypothalamic dysfunction (principally manifested by hypothermia), and ambiguous genitalia in chromosomal males 
        * X-linked lissencephaly with ambiguous genitalia (XLAG)
        * Lissencephaly in XLAG patients, as observed by MRI, progressed in a posterior to anterior gradient of severity. The cortex was only of moderate thickness and the corpus callosum was absent, distinguishing it from X-linked lissencephaly caused by mutations in the doublecortin gene (DCX), which is characterized by a very thickened cortex and an anterior to posterior gradient of lissencephaly.
    * Bonneau : global neurologic impairment, perinatal onset of seizures, and temperature instability, .....
* West syndrome (X-linked infantile spasms)
