## Introduction 
* 2 hyp
    * inter-hemispheric independence hypothesis : during evolution, brain size expansion led to functional lateralisation in order to avoid excessive conduction delays between the hemispheres. Accordingly, functionally lateralised regions will be connected less strongly via the corpus callosum than non-lateralised regions to make processing of lateralised functions more efficient. 
    * inter-hemispheric competition hypothesis : functional lateralisation arises from the competition between the hemispheres that inhibit each other via the corpus callosum. As functionally lateralised regions would need to inhibit the opposite hemisphere more than non-lateralised regions, they could be more connected by the corpus callosum
