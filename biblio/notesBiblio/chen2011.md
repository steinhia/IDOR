# Structural MRI in Autism Spectrum Disorder
 
## Resume 
* short Review of fMRI studies
* ROI : ASD children have increased brain and amygdala volume, decreased CC volume ....
* DTI : reduced FA, increased mean diffusivity, increased radial diffusivity in the CC
* many inconsistencies in methodology
 
## Introduction 
* voxel-based, sufaced-based, tensor-based morphometry and diffusion-tensor imaging
* Core features of ASD:
    * 1) impairment in reciprocal social interactions; 
    * 2) verbal and nonverbal communication deficits;
    * 3) repetitive and ritualized behaviors; and
    * 4) a narrow range of interests. 
    * 5) 30% mental retardation
*  diffusion-tensor imaging (DTI) assesses the direction of water diffusion for each voxel; 
    * sensitive to loss of white-matter (WM) integrity and to differences in connectivity. 

## Structural MRI in ASD
* classified in :
    * region-ofinterest-based
        * semi-manual -> depends on chosen areas, but increase statistical power
        * contrary to voxel-wise analysis methods, when no consensus
        * 5-10% larger brains -> linked to grey and white matter (not ventricular volumes), unclear if persists through life
        * CC : reduced volume in anterior middle and posterior in juveniles and adults
        * increased amygdala volume (only earlier subjects)
    * density-based analysis
        * focus on relative concentrations of GM and WM structures in spatially normalized images
        * decreased GM density in frontal, temporal
        * decrease WM density temporal
    * VBM-based 
        * tissue density and tissue volume
        * difficult to aggregate different studies at the voxel level (different pipeline)
        * more frequent : decreased GM density (even if sometimes increase...)
        * increased GM in frontal, temporal, parietal lobes + limbic system
        * decreased WM volumes frontal, temporal + limbic system
      -> increase GM volume, decrease density
      -> WM both decrease
    * SBM-based
        * topographic measurements
        * changes in frontal, temporal, parietal, not occipital -> direction inconsistent
        * increased cortical thickness in parietal lobes
    * TBM-based.
        * avantage méthodo: less false positive
        * decreased GM volumes in parietal, left temporal, left occipital
      
* Longitudinal studies
    * schumann 2023 (1.5y until 5y) : abnormal all except occipital
    * hardan2024 : brain volume and cortical thickness
        * GM greater decrease in volume over time for ASD
      
* DTI (Diffusion tensor imaging)
    * WM integrity by looking how water diffuses within each voxel
    * measures fractional anisotropy (spatial symmetry of diffusion) and 
    * apparent diffusion coefficient : degree of restriction of diffusion
    * less frequent : mean diffusivity and radial diffusivity
    * Alexander : reduced FA, increased mean diffusivity, increased radial diffusivity in the CC
        * suggests microstructure of the CC affected in ASD
    * Barnea-Goraly : DTI to investigate WM integrity
        * reduced FA in brain areas theory of mind and social cognition + CC
    * consistently reduced FA in CC, across broad age range

* Diagnostic models of ASD based of MRI features
    * ML classifier based on images
    * good sensitivity, specificity and accuracy
    * example : Jiao2018 : thickness-based > volume-based models
