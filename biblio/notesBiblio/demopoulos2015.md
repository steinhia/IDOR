## Typical development of the corpus callosum
* end of the first trimester: neurons in layers II, III and V of the neocortex send projections that migrate medially, cross the midline and form ‘homotopic’ connections with their anatomic match in the contralateral hemisphere
* While the fibers of the corpus callosum (CC) are established by birth, maturation of the axonal structure continues through adolescence.
    * 90% organisation avant 11 ans
* critical development events
    * midline formation 
    * generation of callosal neurons (growth regulated by midline glial structures)
    * innervation to their targets
* Events regulated by chemical signals -> implicated in disorders in CC
    * gene and protein NF2

## Function of the CC
* From anterior to posterior, these divisions include :
    * the rostrum (prefrontal cortex) 
        * implicated in motor function, inhibitory control and emotion regulation
        * diminuted rostrum region associated with ADHD
    * genu (frontal and inferior parietal)
        * role in integrating incoming sensory information with cognitive control and planning
        * including TOC and ruminative thoughts
        * injury = balance and mobility difficulty
    * rostral body (parietal)
        * role in sensorimotor integration and higher order visuospatial processing
            * rapid transmission of sensory-motor information
        * stronger visuospatial performance has been found to be associated with increased structural integrity measured by FA in the body of the CC
        * also integration of visual and verbal information.
    * anterior midbody, 
    * posterior midbody, 
    * isthmus (temporal and posterior parietal)
        * slower connections via the isthmus may allow for maintaining intrahemispheric inhibition (IHI) 
            * potentially via the GABAergic system
            * IHI plays role in language specialization
    * anterior splenium (parietal and temporal lobes, small diamater fibers)
    * posterior splenium (occipital lobes, large diameter fibers)
        * In neurotypical adolescents, increased structural connectivity in the splenium is positively corre- lated with visual-motor speed, expressive vocabulary and single-word reading
