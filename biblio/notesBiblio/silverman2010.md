## Introduction 
* 90% monozygots twins
* rare variants (both de novo and familiar) and epigenetic factors
* Neurotransmission genes, including the serotonin transporter, oxytocin and vasopressin receptors and GABA (γ-aminobutyric acid) receptor subunit β3, have been repeatedly associated with autism or highly implicated in social and affiliative behaviours impaired in autism
* Mouse models with construct validity are being used to evaluate hypotheses about both genetic and environmental causes of autism, including single gene polymorphisms, copy number variants, epigenetic modifications, environmental toxins, prenatal infections, immune dysfunctions and mitochondrial abnormalities
 
## Tests used on mouses
* reciprocal social interactions : sniffing, pushing, physical contact ... using videotapes
* social approach : time spent in chamber with other mouse VS chamber with novel object 
* partition test : chamber separated in 2 : can hear, see, smell but no physical contact -> time spent near the separation
* social (partner) preference test : familiar vs novel
* social transmission of food preference : obtain meaningful information (food flavor) from social interaction
* no possibility to test theory of mind, humor sense...
* physical disabilities : false positive (low sniffing etc) -> general evaluation
