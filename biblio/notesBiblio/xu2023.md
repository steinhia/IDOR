# Divergent social communication between autistic and non-autistic individuals revisited: unraveled via an Integrated Model of Pragmatic Competence
 
## Resume 
* Integrated Model of Pragmatic Competence (IMPC) to clarify the main factors leading to the divergent social interactions ASD/non-ASD
 
## Introduction 
* ASD : persistent sociopragmatic inadequacy
* most common perspective view : cognitive deficit
* Kissine2021 
    * usually intrinsic link between ToM and use of language is weak
    * challenges "constructivism" : language learned through communication
    * supports "nativism" : languages relies on internal biological mechanisms, independantly of social interaction
* Chomsky : grammatics and pragmatics work together
* pragmatic competence : ability to use language appropriately in real contexts — understanding intentions, context, social norms, etc
* Mao2023 Integrative Model of Pragmatic Competence 
    * Internal Pragmatic Competence (IPC) : abstract reasoning, syntax, semantics, etc, intact on ASD
    * Pragmatic Competence for External Communication (PCEC) : bad communication btw external social context and intern modules on ASD
        * "egocentric", non-interactive persons
        * adapt to others in conversation, understand intentions, feelings, use context-appropriate language
        * necessitates functional internal modules capable of adapting to exterior context
* Mitchell2021 : neurotypic influence ASD perception and behavior -> necessity to explore relationship btwn mental health and P/B
* Lopez : understand others not only depend on analysis of social information (gestures, intentions..)
    * better to adopt the "second-person" approach : contribute directly to interaction, not only intern analysis of the situation
    * // DEP : social interactions should be reevaluated from the perspective of both parties of social communications
* ToM approach doesn't place interaction at the core of communication
* goal : learn "shared interactional expertise" -> which subcomponents ??
* analysis of PCEC in terms of both sides ???

* demande pragmalinguistique : demande exprimée via le langage qui ne se limite pas à sa structure ou son sens littéral, mais qui dépend de la capacité à interpréter des intentions implicites
* "tu as oublié l'eau dans le sac" -> structure syntaxique générée par la compétence grammaticale -> proposition sémantique
    * -> utilisation connaissances pragmatiques -> comprend la demande pragmalinguistique implicite
    * prend pas en compte l’intention communicative de l’interlocuteur (dimension inter-subjective)
    * demande compris à travers IPC et pas PCEC
* "ton sac pèse une tonne" -> raisonnement abductif (cause la plus probable) -> sens implicite
    * != plainte/difficulté
