## Introduction 
* speech : articulatory network, parietal/temporal lobe in the ventral stream
* language : lexical repr, inferior and anterior temporal cortex
* but the 2 are functionally intertwined
* SMA : control mechanism ? timing control, automatization, monitoring, task switching
    * interface between cortical areas (output) and subcortical networks (input, basal ganglia, cerebellum, thalamic structures)
    * pre-SMA / SMA separated by vertical line formed by the anterior commisure
    * neighboring anterior cingular cortex
    * functionally : anterior gradient that varies with complexity of the task
    * relai de basal ganglie/cerbellum vers SMA via the thalamus
*  Pre-SMA is linked to regions in prefrontal cortex,  inferior  frontal gyrus (IFG), angular gyrus, and anterior cingulate cortex whereas the cluster of the SMA  proper comprises connections to premotor, primary motor, somatosensory, and the middle  cingulate cortex (Kim et al., 2010). 
* rs-fmri : SMA-propor more to motor cortex, pre-SMA to cognitive linguistic processes
* Furthermore, pre-SMA shows white matter connectivity to the  cingulate cortex via the cingular bundle, which seems to be functionally associated to the  processing of response conflicts (Yamamoto et al., 2015). 
* clinically, speech disorder after left SMA lesion, rather than right
    * impaired initiation of sequence of articulation
    * maybe temporary : right hemisphere takes the turn
    * pre-sma involved in integration between the 2 hemispheres
