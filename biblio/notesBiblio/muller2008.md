## Etude 
Social experiences : 6 major themes
1) intense isolation, 
2) difficulty initiating social interactions (how to initiate)
3) challenges relating to communication (irony, sarcasm etc)
4) longing for intimacy and social connectedness (even deeper friendships)
    * desire for greater closeness, and hesitancy and fear about what that might mean
5) desire to contribute to one’s community, and 
6) effort to develop greater social/self-awareness -> develop social skills (talk too much, too many details..)

Recommendations for social supports
(1) external supports, 
* joint focus and shared interest activities; 
    * thematically focused interactions based primarily on the exchange of information
    * shared focus on activity that does not require interaction (TV..)
* structured social activities;
    * like danse class, high predictability but provide high sense of community
* small groups and dyads; 
    * natural laboratories to study dynamics
    * better when goal-oriented
* facilitated social interactions and opportunities for ‘tracking off’ others; 
* opportunities to observe/model socially appropriate behaviors.
(2) communication supports, 
* alternative modes of communication (notes, internet...)
* explicit/clear/specific communication
* instruction of social cues (how to tell compliments..)
(3) self-initiated supports, 
* physical/outdoors/creative activities
  * improvization helps training social skills
* alone time
(4) attitudinal supports.
