# Agenesis of the Corpus Callosum in California 1983–2003: A Population-Based Study
 
## Resume 
* The objective of this study was to characterize the prevalence, demographic risk factors, and malformations associated with agenesis and hypoplasia of the corpus callosum diagnosed in infancy.
* prevalence 1.8/10000 maybe underestimated
 
## Introduction 
* AgCC frequently associated with other central nervous malformations and/or cognitive impairments (visual processing, social..)
* sometimes asymptomatic
* wang2004 : 1:1000 prevalence estimation
 
## Etude 
* excluded children with complex central nervous system (CNS) malformations
* diagnosed using varying methods (MRI, Pet, head ultrasound, head surgery or autopsy..)
* when genetic anomaly, 6x > when advances maternal age (less paternal age)
* more AgCC when premature
* chromosomic abnormality detected in 17% cases
* infants with also chromosomic abnormalities more likely to have also cardiac, musculoskeletic and gastrointestinal abnormalities
* fMRI infants : 49% had also CNS malformations
* abnormal cerebellar vermis (pont) 11%
* co-occurrence of CNS can also reglect better diagnostic of most severe cases
