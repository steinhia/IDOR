## Hyperfocus and flow
* conditions for entering flow
    * reachable challenge (3)
    * immediate feedback (3)
* description of flow
    * intensed concentration (1)
    * merging of action and awareness (2)
    * loss of awareness of oneself as a social actor (2)
    * a sense that one can in principle deal with the situation because one knows how to respond to whatever happens next (4)
    * distortion of temporal experience (faster) (2)
    * experience of the activity as intrinsically rewarding (3)

* fun/interesting task
    * Flow short scale
    * more flow when skill-demand comparability
    * more flow when "hope of success" than "fear of failure"
    * more flow when perceived importance of the task
    * suggests "rewarding" as a good criterion
* intense state of concentration/diminished perception of the environment
    * attention required for conscious awareness -> as attention is a resource, not allocated tothat during flow
* improved performance
    * 3 conditions : boredom, fit, overload
    * similar levels of cortisol during fit and overload -> physiologically more focused than necessary leading to better perf ?
    * but flow -> high performance or the opposite ??
        * no causal relationship because cannot create flow, can measure co-occurrence flow with fit condition and performance
    * maybe only more attentional resources + feeling better performance -> faster processing of info
    * ease of processing heuristic : info that is processed quickly is judged to be learned better than more slowly processed information.
        * maybe just an impression, regardless whather actually succeed in the task
      
* neural correlates
    * th dietrich (2004) : info processing = explicit and implicit processing 
        * flow = only implicit processing / explicit reduced "transitory hypofrontality" ?
    * critic : hyperfocus is also a question of attention / not only prefrontal cortex
        * castelar 2019 : video game (flow, too hard, too easy) + simple auditive task (to measure attention switching)
            * later frontal activity -> no absence but rather delay, because less prioritary
            * but CAUTION : frontal activity does not inherently imply the engagement of attentional control
    * weber2009 : hyperfocus is the result of synchro between attentional and reward networks (balance between skill and diff)
        * but not really supported by data..

## Hyperfocus and being "in the zone"
* low variance = better accuracy
* not the same thing as hyperfocus
* more higher range than distinct state

## Definition of hyperfocus
* (1) induced by task engagement
* (2) characterized by an intense state of sustained or selective attention
* (3) diminished perception of non-task relevant stimuli
* (4) task performance improves

## Hyperfocus and psychiatric disorders

* ADHD
    * attention AND executive functions
    * inattentive and hyperactive, or combined
    * engage in tasks for times longer than typical
    * controls : alpha/beta frontal activation increased during the game, decreased for ADHD -> necessitated less cognitive effort
        * alpha = inhibition des distractions / 
        * beta = effort cognitif actif : dans ADHD engagement automatique, pas besoin de alpha -> légère baisse
        * delta peut refléter immersion intense/récompense intrinsèque
* ASD
    * definition hyperfocus in ASD : difficulties in shifting attention, disengaging from details (geurts2009)
    * no studies...
