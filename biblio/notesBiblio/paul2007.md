# Agenesis of the Corpus Callosum: Genetic, Developmental and Functional Aspects of Connectivity
 
## Resume 
 
## Introduction 
* CC contient connexions homotopiques (connections régions équivalentes entre les 2 hémisphères) et hétérotopiques (régions diff)
* nature de la communication entre les 2 hémisphères :
    * IHT interhemispheric transfer
    * hyp excitatrice ? intégration des infos des 2 hémisphères -> hyp favorisée, CC favorise le partage d'info
    * hyp inhibitrice ? chacun sa propre tâche/rendre indépendant
* hypogenesis : partial absence
* Corpus callosum formation involves multiple steps, including :
    * correct midline patterning -> sert de pont pour les axones commissuraux
    * formation of telencephalic hemispheres -> se placent d'une part et d'autre de la ligne médiane
    * birth (neurogenesis) and specification (know that they will project on the other hemisphere) of commissural neurons 
      * neurons that will project on the CC
    * axon guidance across the midline 
      * attracted into the median line by some signals (guidance of glial cells, attractive or repulsive molecules to attract them)
      * first neurons to cross are from the cingular cortex (emotional regulation and attention), puis neocortex
        * axones pionniers émettent récepteur de guidance
    * to their final target in the contralateral hemisphere -> target region connected to the other hemisphere
* multiple glial cells present in the midline
    * glial wedge (cone glial) -> guide les axones dans la bonne direction (empêche rentrer dans le septum, guide à travers..)
    * midline zipper (glies de fermeture, fusionnent avec la ligne médiane, important pour la traversée des axones)
    * indusium griseum IGG (guidance + molécules de signalisation)
    * FGFR1 : protein responsible for formation of glial cells in the midline (proliferation, differntiation, migration of IGG)
* fibres fines (peu myélinisées) : infos sémantiques, conceptuelles, émotionnelles (non-literal..)
  * plus souvent genou
* fibres épaisses (très myélinisées) : coordination sensorimotrice fine, synchronisation visuelle/motrice rapide
  * plus souvent splenium/tronc
* genetics : identifiable cause for only 30-45%
    * 10% chromosomic anomalies
    * 20-35% recognizable genetic syndrome (only 10-15% for complete agenesis -> 75% without identifiable cause)
    * combination of genetic mechanisms, including 
        * single-gene Mendelian (héréditaire), 
          * XLAG (gene ARX)
          * syndromeCRASH, gène L1CAM (code protéine d'adhésion cellulaire très présente dans le cerveau)
          * gènes DCC et GAP-43 -> guidage des cellules
              * hétérozygote (AgCC partielle) ou homozygote (complète + autres anomalies)
          * Syndrome d'Andermann : gène SLC12A6 ..
          * Syndrome Meckel Gruber
        * single-gene sporadic mutations (sans antécédent connu, de novo ou porteur sans symptome)
            * Mowat-Wilson syndrom, syndrome d'Aicardi, ...
        * complex genetics (which may have a mixture of inherited and sporadic mutations) 
    * lien avec autisme et schizophrénie
* environmental factors
    * alcool in utero perturbe formation cellules gliales (gliogenese)
* Behavioral impairment
    * Disparate manifestations -> difficult to group into homogeneic groups
* Connectivity deficits
    * could pass through anterior commissure, sometimes mild symptoms but still present, particularly complex tasks
    * cause of deficits ?
        * normal lateralization (even exaggerated ?)
        * problem wouldnot come at the localization, but communication between 2 hemispheres -> callosal disconnection hypothesis
* Neuropsychological impairment
    * voir paul2003
    * poor at using context to infer meaning brown2005
* Neuropsychiatric disorders
    * linked to autism, but also schizophrenia
    * TDAH have perturbed motor inhibition, which is usually regulated by CC
    * AgCC, like many psychiatric disorders, but unlike callosotomy, results from abnormal development of connectivity.
* Integration of multiple disciplines
    * builing an endophenotype "intermediary traits" between genetics and phenotype
    * absence of CC -> directly linked to the symptoms ?
        * in AgCC additional neuroanatomical factors such as Probst Bundles (amas de fibres aml orientées), colpocephaly, abnormal ipsilateral connections (du même côté) and abnormal cortical folding may well contribute separately to the clinical outcome of AgCC patients
