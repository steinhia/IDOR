# The Neuropsychological Syndrome of Agenesis of the Corpus Callosum
 
## Resume 
The cores syndrome includes: 
(1) Reduced interhemispheric transfer of sensory-motor information; 
(2) Reduced cognitive processing speed; 
(3) Deficits in complex reasoning and novel problem-solving. 
These domains do not appear to reflect different neuroanatomical abnormalities, but rather different domains of expression of reduced interhemispheric communication from callosal absence.
 
## Introduction 
* disruption between 7 and 20 week of pregnancy
* 1/4000 births
* review AgCC without other neuro dysmorphology

## Diminished Interhemispheric Integration of Sensory-Motor Information
* no full disconnection syndrom contrary to commissurotomy
* interhemispheric integration of easy stimuli, but not more difficult tasks
    * reconnaissance d'objets dans les 2 champs visuels
    * tactual-spatial information : Tactual Performance test and Finger localization test (Dunn2000)
* Bimanual Coordination Test (BCT) -> bimanual motor coordination slower and less accurate (mueller2009)
        * but similar to neurotypical developing children with CC notfully developped (marion2003)
        * neurotypical adults : poor structural integrity of motor connections via CC associated with poor bimanual coordination (gooijers2013)

## Reduced Speed of Cognitive Processing
* Processing speed is highly vulnerable to disruptions in white matter connectivity, particularly the CC (solmaz2017)
* deficient cognitive inhibition and flexibility in adults with AgCC
    * but differences in cognitive control primary the results of diff in processing speed (marco2012)
    * impairment in processing speed exacerbated when higher task demand (mathias2004)

## Difficulty with Complex Processing
* interhemispheric resources are recruited to assist with cognitively complex tasks (koivisto2000)
* benefit of interhemispheric processing decreases with practice (cherbuin2005)
* no deficits in overlearned cognitive processes (erickson2013)
* impairment in abtract reasoning (brown2000), concept formation (fischer1992), problem-solving (brown2012), generalization (solursh1965)
* schieffer2000 : impaired for more complex tasks

## Associated Cognitive and Psychosocial Deficiencies
* difficulties encoding verbal and visual information in memory and spontaneously retrieving newly learned information (erickson2014)
* exerting cognitive inhibition and flexibility (marco2012)
* formulating strategies (brown2012)
* recognizing emotion in faces (bridgman2014)
* difficulty reasoning abstractly about emotions in social context (anderson2017)
* imagining and inferring the mental, emotional, and social functioning of others (kang2009)
* expressing emotions in words (pazienza2011)
* awareness of functional deficits (kaplan2012)
* schieffer2000 : difficulties only with older children
