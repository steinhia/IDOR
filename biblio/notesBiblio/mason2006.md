## Resume 
* dynamic recruitment of cortical networks in response to text constraints

* 3 theories role RH
    * the spillover of processing to other differential specialized RH networks in response to LH capacity utilization 
    * RH used during integration of inferences with the text
    * coarse coding theory : inferences result from activation of coarsely coded semantic information in the RH.
Parallel Networks of Discourse:
1. Basic LH Sentence Network (Not Uniquely a Part of Discourse Processing) -> several LH regions
   * syntactic structure, word meaning, visual/graphemic, phono and lexical-semantic processing
2. A coarse semantic processing network (right middle and superior temporal)
   * activation of coarse meaning and inferences for each word, looks for overlap between other activated
3. A coherence monitoring network (bilateral dorsolateral prefrontal)
   * increase activity when lack of coherence or unusually semantic field -> helps finding coherence / inferences to integrate
4. A text integration network (left inferior frontal-left anterior temporal)
   * helps construct the "situation/mental model of the text"
5. A network for interpreting a protagonist’s or agent’s perspective (bilateral medial frontal/posterior right temporal/parietal)
   * understanding other's plans/motivations / emotion-based memories
6. A spatial imagery network (left dominant, bilateral intraparietal sulcus)
   * high-imageries sentences or metaphors

2 discourse theories : The coarse coding theory (Beeman, 1998) the spillover of processing theory (Just et al., 1996)
 
## Introduction 
* several activated networks, beyond word and sentence comprehension
* traditional left hemisphere language network (left hemisphere inferior frontal gyrus, the superior and middle temporal gyrus, potions of the inferior temporal gyrus, and the angular gyrus)

* new discourse theories :
    * the coarse coding theory of right hemisphere processing (Beeman, 1998)
      * activations de significations plus larges (moins littérales, moins fréquentes) et peu saillantes -> implicit/ambigu
    * the dynamic recruitment of networks in response to text constraints (Ferstl, Rinck & von Cramon, 2005; Mason & Just, 2004)
      * recrute dynamiquement différents réseaux selon les contraintes du texte : cohérence, inférence, perspective..
    * a Theory of Mind system responsible for awareness of different perspectives (Gallagher & Frith, 2003)
    * the spillover of processing to other differential specialized networks in response to capacity utilization (Just, Carpenter, Keller, Eddy, & Thulborn 1996)
* more traditional discourse theories
    * Kintsch’s Construction-Integration framework (1988)
      * first creation of many inferences, during construction of global representation of the text
      * then integration of those linked to the text's representation/pruning of contradictions
    * Gernsbacher’s Structure Building Framework (1990)
      * adding/modification of basis (discourse's topic) -> topic at the end = greater shift 
      * left temporal region = building the foundation at the beginning of the text
      * right temporal region = "fill in" infos, complete the topic
    * Myers and O’Brien’s Resonance model (1998)
      * resonance of information stored in LTM if pertinent
    * Giora’s Graded Salience Hypothesis (1997)
      * first activation of more salient (litteral/frequent)
    * van den Broek’s Landscape architecture (1996)
      * discourse comprehension guided by the most active/accessible info (activation depends of context and reader's intention)

* PROTAGONIST INTERPRETER
    * ToM : medial frontal gyrus 
        * understanding of other's intentions, metaphors, moral judgement, identification of thematic roles within a story

* Proposition of networks
    * dynamic recruitment of cortical networks in response to text constraints
    * the spillover of processing to other differential specialized networks in response to capacity utilization
    * Basic LH Sentence Network (Not Uniquely a Part of Discourse Processing) -> several LH regions
        * syntactic structure, word meaning, visual/graphemic, phono and lexical-semantic processing
    * Coarse RH Semantic Processing Network -> right temporal region
        * activation of coarse meaning and inferences for each word, looks for overlap between other activated
    * Dorsolateral Prefrontal Coherence Monitor Network
        * increase activity when lack of coherence or unusually semantic field -> helps finding coherence inference to integrate
        * This iterative process continues until coherence is achieved or a complete breakdown in comprehension occurs.
    * Left Frontal – Temporal Text Integration Network
        * the left inferior frontal gyrus and the left anterior superior temporal sulcus region
        * helps construct the "situation/mental model of the text"
        * can pass to right if overload
    * Medial Frontal gyrus : Protagonist/Agent Interpreter Network
        * understanding other's plans/motivations
        * emotion-based memories
    * Intraparietal Sulcus Spatial Network
        * high-imageries sentences or metaphors
