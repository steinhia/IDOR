## Resume : cellular mechanisms implicated in autism
* glutamate receptors : excitotoxicity (excessive stimulation -> damaged synapses)
* GABA signalisation : migration or synaptic connection -> desequilibre inhibition/excitation
* cerebral growing : variants in genes related to synaptic structure, adhesion molecules
* glial cells : more glial cells in ASD, more pro-inflammatory moleculs

 
## Discussion 
three groups of genes that may be involved in the pathophysiology of ASD: 
(1) neurotransmitter receptors (ex. : sérotonine, dopamine, GABA...)
(2) genes involved in neurotransmitter metabolism (ex. : tryptophane → sérotonine, mélatonine)
(3) cell adhesion molecules and scaffolding proteins (adhésion cellulaire, échafaudage)
