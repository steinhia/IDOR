* overfocused attention may be confounded with inattention -> overfocusing scale to differentiate
* Kinsbourne attributed autistic symptomatology to fluctuatingly exaggerated ascending activation of the cortex from the brainstem. 
  * brainstem (tronc cérébral) handles automatic (breathing, balance) and sensory inputs
* Overfocused attention may be related to abnormal neurotransmitter activity.  
  * Kinsbourne (1991) linked overfocusing to dopamine overreactivity. 
  * excessive dopamin linked to stereotyped behavior and overreactivity
* overarousal hyp reformulated into "noisy firing of neurons"
  * hussmann (2001) suggests lack of inhibition (gaba-ergic neurons)
  * results inhyper-excitation of target neurons and diff in distinguishing a stim from competing noise
  * Overreactivity would reflect this overexcitement of sensory systems. 
  * Overfocus and perseveration would be compensatory behaviors, serving to reduce sensory input into a narrow, controllable scope. 
  * overreaction could be a consequence of impossibility to switch attention from aversive stimulus
    * Switching attention involves dorsolateral prefrontal cortex and cerebellum
* Casanova 2002 : microcellular columns in the brain (post-mortem) smaller and more numerous
  * cons : overconnected neural network, with a loss in selectivity
* underreactivity the same as overreactivity (not under the focus of attention)
