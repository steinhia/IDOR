# An MRI Study of the Corpus Callosum in Autism
 
## Resume 
* After controlling for total brain volume, gender, and perform ance IQ , the authors detected a significantly smaller size of the body and posterior subregions of the corpus callosum in the autistic individuals.
* In the contex t of recent reports of increased brain size in autism, several possible mechanisms are considered in exploring the significance of a smaller relative size of the corpus callosum in autism.
 
## Introduction 
* CC used to study abnormal connectivity in the brain
* hypothesis comes from neuro and psycho studies
* abnormal posterial brain enlargement ? smaller frontal lobe ? inconsistencies!
* pattern of fibers traversing CC are viewed to reflect anterior-posterior pattern of connectivity of the cortical regions
* portions of CC map to cortical regions, anterior maps to anterior, posterior maps to posterior..
 
## Etude 
* anterior, body and posterior CC
* 35 autists, >= 12y
* Wechsler inteeligence scale, or Leiter scale -> IQ
* controls matched for age and IQ
* BRAIN BLAST for surface and volume rendering
* alignement des plans
* manual segmentation of ROI
* Witelson method to divide CC into 7 subregions -> here only 3
* adjustment for total brain size, IQ, gender

## Results
* body and posterior CC smaller than controls -> only adjusted size
* Egaas et al, not adjusted but still had difference in posterior
    * interpreted as being related to hypoplasia of parietal lobes in autism
    * on aurait pu faire l'inverse de l'hyp parietal lobe bigger, CC posterior smaller
* Filipek : no specific subregions
* Berthier : posterior thinning
* Guerin : idem single post-mortem study
