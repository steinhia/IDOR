# Agenesis of the Corpus Callosum: Clinical and Genetic Study in 63 Young Patients

## Resume 
* reports the clinical features of 63 patients with agenesis of the corpus callosum who received in-depth genetic, clinical, and laboratory testing
* 30 complete/33 partial AgCC
* Other associated nervous system malformations
* genetics : Cytogenetically detectable chromosomal abnormalities (7 patients) and subtelomeric rearrangements (3 patients) 
* Neuromotor skills were impaired in almost all cases (58/63). Mental retardation of different severity was present in 52 cases, whereas 2 patients were borderline and 9 patients had normal intelligence quotient
* Chromosomal anomalies were present in 7 patients (11%)
* AgCC related to a broad range of clinical manifestations
 
## Introduction 
* AgCC : 3-7/1000 general population, 2-3% children with developmental disabilities
* extreme genetic heterogeneity : several mechanisms might be responsible for callosal agenesis
    * Genotype-phenotype correlation studies can contribute to identifying subpopulations with common feature
* other central nervous system malformations :
    * complete AgCC : 10
        * mainly malformations of cortical development resulting from altered neuronal proliferation or migration processes
        * macroscopic alterations of cortical organization
    * partial 8=42%
        * malformations (8 cases) mainly involved the posterior fossa with the brainstem (cerebellum, brainstem, and ventricular system
* Non CNS malformations
    *  Cranio-facial abnormalities (65%), skeletal abnormalities (31%), cardiac pathologies (27%), ocular abnormalities (22%), genital (4%) and renal abnormalities (2%) 
* 35% epilepsy : higher when associated with other CNS malformations
* isolated agenesis less frequent (17%) -> mais biais de sélection..
* retard mental lourd : 4-7% porteurs d’aneuploïdies (anomalies nombre de copies ou d'un segment d'un chr.) chromosomiques dans les régions subtélomériques (extrémités des chromosomes)
    * AgCC dans cette étude -> 5%
* partial/complete AgCC have similar amount of other cerebral malformations, but different type
    * partial AgCC -> correlated with posterior fossa malformations
    * complete AgCC -> more frequently associated with malformations of cortical development
* frequent association with craniofacial abnormalities
* no unique prognosis, from normal cognitive level to severe psychomotor delay
