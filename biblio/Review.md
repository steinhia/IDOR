### General model for hypotheses on autism (inspired from Rubenstein2003)
  * 1. Precoce genetic or environmental alteration (gene mutation or atypical environment)
  * 2. Less GABAergic neurons in cortex/amygdale (less produced or migrated)
  * 3. Less inhibition (GABA) compared to excitation (glutamate) = déséquilibre excitation/inhibition
  * 4. Excessive Plasticity (too much LTP, freezed circuits) -> learning too fast and less flexible, overreactivity to stimuli
  * 5. underdifferentiated representations, simple stimuli activate large populations of neurons
  * 6. Local excess excitability -> less differentiation -> high distance lack of excitability -> lower connectivity
  * 7. Atypical behavior like in autism (hypersensitivity, rigid memory, less flexibility, increased risk of epilepsy)

## Role of the CC
* banich 1998
  * CC often conceived as a mechanism for transferring sensory information and coordinating processing between the hemisphere
  * also role in attentional processing, because allows division of labor between hemispheres (more computational power)
    * dynamic interactions increase the processing capacity of the brain
    * outcome depends on the balance between a better computational power and computational cost for integration info
    * 2 hyp : either "open a gates" when processing capacity exceeding in one hemisphere, or CC attentional module that affects attention
  * in 2 attention conceptions :
    * as a resource
    * as a selective mechanism for gating sensory information
* yuan 2020 :
  * Increased intrahemispheric structural/functional connections -> compensatory mechanisms in AgCC ?
  * hyp compensation : could be that AgCC become more lateralized to compensate for bad IH connectivity
* hinkley 2016 :
  * pAgCC : bilateralization of language / cAgCC right lateralization of language
  * loss left lateralization directed linked to low VIQ -> clinical consequences for atypical lateralization
  * some regions not directly linked to language less activated in the LH
    * compensation strategy would come at the expense of processing in these regions
  * supports hyp role CC : transmit information across cortical fields to suppress one area while the contralateral hemisphere is active
* karolis 2019 : 
  * hyp lateralization (inter-hemispheric independence hypothesis) : during evolution, brain size expansion led to functional lateralisation in order to avoid excessive conduction delays between the hemispheres -> functionally lateralised regions will be connected less strongly via the corpus callosum than non-lateralised regions to make processing of lateralised functions more efficient.
  * but limits recuperation capacity after brain lesions
* gazzaniga2000 : role of the CC in lateralization -> evolutionary theory
  * new function (language) in one hemisphere, the other continues to perform old functions
    * lateralization = dev of new skills, maintenance of others
    * non-lateralized = more efficient, parallel processing thanks to redundancy (but needs coordination/integration from CC)
  * the CC has enabled the specialization of systems (some of them are lateralized, some of them still have redundancy) :
    * LH : language and problem-solving, RH : facial recognition, attention monitoring
    * memory : LH more memory of words, RH more memory of faces, objects for both -> more stimulus than memory type (sem/epis)
    * attention : endogenous lateralized LH / exogenous independantly in each hemisphere
    * coordination : split-brain can perform independant tasks with 2 arms, 2 visual searchs, but not 2 decisions (taken successively)

        * can have 2 independant motor plannings (no need for coordination)

## Cognitive science in general 

### Discourse theory (mason 2006)
* dynamic recruitment of cortical networks in response to text constraints
* old discourse theories (coarse coding, construction integration framework, salience hypothesis...)
* 3 theories role RH
  * the spillover of processing to other differential specialized RH networks in response to LH capacity utilization
  * RH used during integration of inferences with the text
  * coarse coding theory : inferences result from activation of coarsely coded semantic information in the RH.
    Parallel Networks of Discourse:
1. Basic LH Sentence Network (Not Uniquely a Part of Discourse Processing) -> several LH regions
  * syntactic structure, word meaning, visual/graphemic, phono and lexical-semantic processing
2. A coarse semantic processing network (right middle and superior temporal)
  * activation of coarse meaning and inferences for each word, looks for overlap between other activated
3. A coherence monitoring network (bilateral dorsolateral prefrontal)
  * increase activity when lack of coherence or unusually semantic field -> helps finding coherence / inferences to integrate
4. A text integration network (left inferior frontal-left anterior temporal)
  * helps construct the "situation/mental model of the text"
5. A network for interpreting a protagonist’s or agent’s perspective (bilateral medial frontal/posterior right temporal/parietal)
  * understanding other's plans/motivations / emotion-based memories
6. A spatial imagery network (left dominant, bilateral intraparietal sulcus)
  * high-imageries sentences or metaphors
